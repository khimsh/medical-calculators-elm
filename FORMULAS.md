## Critical Care & Emergency

This file will be used to store all the formulas used in calculations.

- **Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)**: Eye opening (1-4) + Verbal response (1-5) + Motor response (1-6), total score out of 15
- **APGAR score**: For newborn assessment at 1 and 5 minutes
- **Rule of Nines**: For burn percentage estimation
- **Parkland formula**: Burn resuscitation = 4 mL × kg × % burn (half in first 8 hours)
- **Acute coronary syndrome scores**: HEART score, TIMI score

## Fluid & Electrolytes

- **Maintenance fluid calculation**: 4-2-1 rule (4 mL/kg/hr for first 10 kg, 2 mL/kg/hr for next 10 kg, 1 mL/kg/hr for remaining)
- **Sodium correction rate**: Change in Na⁺ should not exceed 10-12 mEq/L per 24 hours
- **Corrected calcium**: For low albumin = measured Ca + 0.8 × (4 - albumin)
- **Free water deficit**: 0.6 × weight × [(Na/140) - 1]

## Scoring Systems

- **CURB-65**: Pneumonia severity (Confusion, Urea, Respiratory rate, Blood pressure, age ≥65)
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc**: Stroke risk in atrial fibrillation
- **HAS-BLED**: Bleeding risk on anticoagulation
- **Wells criteria**: For DVT/PE probability
- **CHADS₂ score**: Simplified stroke risk assessment

## Obstetrics

- **Estimated due date**: Last menstrual period + 280 days (Naegele's rule)
- Estimated fetal weight formulas
- Bishop score: For cervical favorability

## Drug Conversions

- **Opioid equianalgesic doses**: Converting between morphine, oxycodone, hydromorphone, fentanyl
- Insulin sliding scales and correction factors
- Steroid conversion equivalents

## Quick Reference Values

Normal vital sign ranges by age

- **Pediatric weight estimation**: (Age in years × 2) + 8 = weight in kg
- **ET tube size (pediatric)**: (Age/4) + 4 for uncuffed tubes
- **Quick sepsis criteria (qSOFA)**: Respiratory rate ≥22, altered mentation, SBP ≤100

## Medication Dosing & Administration

- Body Surface Area (BSA): Used for chemotherapy and medication dosing, typically calculated using the Mosteller formula: √[(height in cm × weight in kg) / 3600]
- IV drip rate: (Total volume in mL × drop factor) / Time in minutes
- Dosage calculations: (Desired dose / Available dose) × Quantity

## Cardiac & Hemodynamic

- **Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP)**: [(2 × diastolic) + systolic] / 3, or diastolic + 1/3(pulse pressure)
- **Cardiac Output**: Heart rate × stroke volume
- **Body Mass Index (BMI)**: Weight (kg) / Height (m)²

## Respiratory

- **Tidal volume**: Typically 6-8 mL/kg of ideal body weight for mechanical ventilation
- Oxygen saturation calculations and FiO2 adjustments

## Renal Function

- Creatinine Clearance (Cockcroft-Gault): Estimates kidney function for medication adjustments
- Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR): Using equations like MDRD or CKI-EPI
- Fluid balance: Intake minus output

## Pediatric Dosing

- Weight-based dosing: mg/kg formulas for most pediatric medications

## Other Common Calculations

- Anion gap: (Na⁺) - (Cl⁻ + HCO₃⁻), used to evaluate metabolic acidosis
- Corrected sodium for hyperglycemia
- A-a gradient: For assessing oxygenation problems
